About this gathering

The day before Mother's Day, we gathered

Not every woman in the room was a mother. Not every mother had been well-mothered. We didn't ask anyone to explain their story or justify their presence.

We gave everyone a canvas, some blank pages, and a circle of women who were already there. What came out of it surprised us every time — tenderness, grief, colour, and something that kept feeling like relief.

This is not a class. There is no outcome. You bring what you carry, and you leave a little lighter.

What happens

Three hours. Three things.

01 · Write

A letter

To the mother you needed — whether she was yours, a stranger, or someone you have only imagined. You don't need to send it. You just need to write it.

02 · Paint

Her portrait

Not what she looked like. What she felt like. What she might have given you, in colour, if she had known how.

03 · Be held

In the circle

We share what we made. We witness each other. No explanations required. We close with food, and with the quiet knowledge that we were not alone.

What women said

"I came not knowing what to expect. I left having painted a face I have never seen but somehow always known. Something shifted in that room — quietly, without anyone telling me what to feel. I didn't know I needed this until it was already happening."

Shahla

"Writing the letter was the hardest and the most freeing thing I have done in a long time. I have carried things about my mother for years without a place to put them. That afternoon I finally had somewhere. The painting came out of me like it had been waiting."

Ayleen

"You came here today and you made something. You imagined a mother who saw you clearly, loved you without condition, and gave you what you needed. That imagination lives in you. It always did."
